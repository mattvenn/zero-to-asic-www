faq:
  - q: What does it mean to harden a chip design?
    a: Hardening is the industry term for taking an HDL description and using a tool like LibreLane to convert it into the GDS2 files necessary for manufacture by a foundry.
  - q: What is the input and output of the hardening process?
    a: The input to hardening is an HDL description of the design, and the output is GDS2 files that contain the physical layout ready to send to the foundry.
  - q: What tool is commonly used to harden a design?
    a: LibreLane is the open source tool commonly used to harden a design, automating the steps from RTL through to finished GDS2 files.

Harden is the fancy industry word that means to take your [HDL](/terminology/hdl) and using a tool like [LibreLane](/terminology/librelane) turn it into the [GDS2](/terminology/gds2) files necessary for manufacture by a [foundry](/terminology/foundry).
